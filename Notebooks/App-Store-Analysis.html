Essential to Popular Apps: Analyzing the Success of Best Selling Apps on Google Play Store¶

In this notebook, the goal is to find the best features to attract mobile application users. If we were to develop an app to publish on Google Play Store, what kind of app would be the most successful?

We will analyze existing data about the Google Play Store apps to find the best area to publish a new app. With a focus to maximize user install potential, we'll explore the following:

  • What kind of apps are available in the market?
  • What do users tend to download?

# change size of KB to MB

# remove distracting characters
symbols = ['M', ',', '+', '$']
affected_cols = ['Size', 'Installs', 'Price']
for col in affected_cols:
    # remove non numeric characters in these columns
    for char in symbols:
        data[col] = data[col].str.replace(char, '')
    # convert column to numeric data type
    data[col] = pd.to_numeric(data[col])

What is available in the Market?¶

With almost three million applications available in the Google Play Store, it's a common practice for the market to section off apps based on their category so that users can find what they want more quickly.

In this section, we will answer these following questions:

  • Are there any categories dominating the app store?
  • What categories have the most user download?

Based on recent searches and the fact that the app store has a tab dedicated to games as its own section aside from the other apps, I expect that games will cover a large area of the market.

Over 35% of the apps are for practical use, such as Tools, Business, Productivity, etc. On the other hand, 29% of the apps are in the Family and Game category.



Having a closer look at the Family category, we find that it is a collection of games and educational apps designed for children. As we are living in an intense technology world, kids are exposed to electronics at a young age(often by stealing the phones of their parents!). Similarly, in recent years a lot of games companies have started to expand their products onto mobile platforms to attract new players, which resulted in a lot of new games in the apps market.



About 65% of the market consists of games and productivity apps. But how are the user demands compared to its availability?

What are users into?¶

We've seen from above that apps in the genre of games and practical usage dominated the app market. Since there are so many of these apps, are they what the users want?



In this section, we will look at how each category is doing by measuring the total installs, along with the average number of installs per category.
